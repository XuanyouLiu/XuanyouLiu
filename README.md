# Xuanyou Liu (Zed)

[Website](https://xuanyouliu.com) • [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=FcQWVTYAAAAJ) • [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/xuanyouliu/) • [CV](https://xuanyouliu.com/resume-en/Xuanyou_Liu_Resume.pdf) • [Email](mailto:xuanyou@u.northwestern.edu)

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at **Northwestern University**, advised by [Jessica Hullman](https://users.eecs.northwestern.edu/~jhullman/) and affiliated with the [MU Collective](https://mucollective.northwestern.edu/). My research sits at the intersection of **human-AI interaction, wearable computing, and haptic interfaces**.

I study how intelligent systems and novel sensing technologies can augment human perception and support more effective decision-making. My work spans interactive systems, uncertainty-aware decision support, wearable sensing, electrotactile interfaces, and embedded hardware.

*Previously:* I worked with [Pedro Lopes](https://lab.plopes.org/) at the University of Chicago's HC-Integration Lab. I received my M.S.E. in Robotics from the University of Pennsylvania and my B.E. in Industrial Design from Xi'an Jiaotong University.

## Current Interests

- **Human-AI Interaction** and decision-making under uncertainty
- **Wearable Computing** and novel sensing systems
- **Haptic Interfaces** and electrotactile stimulation
- **EIT-based Hand Pose Estimation** and input sensing
